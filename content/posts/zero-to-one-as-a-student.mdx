Before finance, I co-founded a blockchain logistics venture in Singapore. It didn't become a unicorn. It taught me more than any module.

## Ambiguity is the job

Early-stage work is mostly deciding what to do when nobody can tell you the answer. You learn to make a call, document why, and revisit it without ego.

## Translate, constantly

The hardest skill wasn't technical — it was translating between technical and non-technical stakeholders without losing either. That carries directly into finance operations.

## Why I still build

Neural Edge is the same instinct, pointed at markets: take a messy real-world problem and build the system that makes it legible.

I use AI heavily in my research, but not where most people expect.

## Good: structured review

LLMs are excellent at the boring, structured parts — checking an assumption list against new data, flagging where a thesis and the evidence diverge, summarising a long policy statement into the three things that changed.

## Bad: conviction

The danger is borrowing conviction. A confident summary is not a confident position. I keep the model on the inputs and review side, and keep the decision human.

## The workflow

1. Capture the raw inputs.
2. Let the model normalise and cross-check.
3. Make the call myself.
4. Log it. Review it later, with the model helping spot what I missed.
